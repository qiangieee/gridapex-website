Project Mie

100 MW AI-ready data center campus in Mie, with secured power and strategic access to Japan's major metro markets.

Project Overview

  • Location: Tsu City, Mie Prefecture, Japan; strategically positioned between Tokyo and Osaka
  • Capacity: 100 MW AI-ready colocation campus using modular deployment
  • Site profile: 51,819 m² (approx. 15,675 tsubo) with approx. 28,000 m² usable area after grading
  • Timeline: 100 MW capacity targeted for power-on in 2028-2029
  • Zoning path: Mountainous forest land; rezoning toward Industrial Exclusive use
  • Coverage / FAR: 60% / 200%
  • Connectivity / resilience: Near submarine cable routes; 50m elevation supports strong BCP profile
  • Access: Strong logistics access to technology corridors, Toyota's regional manufacturing base, and Chubu Centrair International Airport

Core Project Advantage — Confirmed Power

  • Confirmed power: 100 MW officially confirmed by Chubu Electric Power.
  • De-risked foundation: Secured power, site control, and rezoning pathway provide a strong execution basis for development.
  • Cost-Efficient Modular Deployment: Modular rollout reduces construction complexity and supports staged delivery toward earlier revenue generation.
  • Mie / Chubu industrial base: Strong regional economy, leading electronics manufacturing presence, and proximity to transmission infrastructure support execution and demand visibility.
  • Market scarcity: 100 MW-scale grid access remains a key bottleneck for AI-ready data center development in Japan.
